# Remote Sensing with Vision-Language Models

## Task Formulations in RS
**Description:** Canonical problem settings defining what models perceive, reason, and output.

### Image-Level Understanding
**Description:** Scene-level categorization or description of entire remote sensing images.

### Object-Level Understanding
**Description:** Localizing and identifying objects using bounding boxes, orientations, or referring expressions.

### Region-Level Understanding
**Description:** Understanding or describing image content at region or subscene spatial extents.

### Pixel-Level and Dense Outputs
**Description:** Producing masks or per-pixel labels via text-conditioned segmentation or classification.

### Temporal and Multimodal Variants
**Description:** Extending tasks to time series, multispectral bands, and cross-modality reasoning.

## RS Dataset Usage and Construction
**Description:** Creating, annotating, and organizing datasets used in remote sensing studies.

### Public Remote Sensing Benchmarks
**Description:** Datasets commonly used as benchmarks for evaluating remote sensing or vision tasks.

### Manual Expert or Crowdsourced Annotation
**Description:** Labels produced through expert interpretation or crowdsourced annotation of remote sensing imagery.

### Rule-Based or GIS/Map-Derived
**Description:** Labels derived from rules, or geospatial layers, maps, or geospatial vector data.

### Synthetic and Simulated RS Imagery
**Description:** Artificial remote sensing imagery or spatial labels generated through sensor or scene simulation.

### Language- or Instruction-Derived RS Supervision
**Description:** Supervision generated by reformulating RS tasks or annotations into question–answer or instruction formats.

### Pairwise or Relational RS Supervision
**Description:** Supervision defined by spatial, temporal, or cross-sensor relationships between remote sensing observations.

## Training and Adaptation Regimes
**Description:** Procedures for adapting, training, and specializing model parameters for RS tasks.

### Prompt-Based Conditioning
**Description:** Using prompts, templates, or in-context examples to steer model behavior without updating model parameters.

### Knowledge- and Context-Augmented
**Description:** Conditioning models with external context such as maps, metadata, or auxiliary text to support RS tasks.

### Instruction Tuning and Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)
**Description:** Updating model parameters using instruction/QA supervision or labeled RS data to improve RS tasks.

### Parameter-Efficient Adaptation
**Description:** Adaptation methods such as LoRA or adapters minimizing trainable parameters during task specialization.

### Hybrid Supervision and Consistency Losses
**Description:** Geometry-guided, cross-modal, or knowledge-guided constraints enforcing agreement during training.

### Knowledge Distillation and Freezing Policies
**Description:** Teacher–student training and selective parameter freezing to retain general capabilities.

### Multi-Task and Joint Training
**Description:** Training models on multiple remote sensing tasks or objectives simultaneously.

### Answer Space Constraints
**Description:** Restricting outputs to predefined classes, counts, ranges, or response sets.

### Structured Spatial Outputs
**Description:** Producing spatially explicit results such as regions, maps, or localized predictions.
