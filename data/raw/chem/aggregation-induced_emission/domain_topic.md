# aggregation-induced emission

## Construction of photoresponsive AIE polymers
**Description:** Strategies combining AIE luminogens with light-responsive units to build functional macromolecules.

### Main-chain copolymerization of difunctional monomers
**Description:** Covalent incorporation of AIE and photoactive motifs along polymer backbones for uniform responsiveness.

### Side-chain copolymerization with monofunctional units
**Description:** Pendant photochromic groups on AIE polymers enable tunable solubility and multistimuli responses.

### Postmodification of polymer precursors
**Description:** Click and controlled radical chemistries graft photoresponsive units onto preformed AIE polymers efficiently.

### In situ and charge-generating polymerizations
**Description:** One-pot formations from inactive monomers, including azonia polyelectrolytes with emergent AIE and photosensitization.

## Supramolecular strategies
**Description:** Noncovalent assembly routes leveraging reversible interactions to integrate AIEgens and photoactive components.

### Host–guest assembly driven systems
**Description:** Cyclodextrin or cucurbituril complexes create light-switchable architectures with reversible emission control.

### Hydrogen-bonded polymer networks and gels
**Description:** Dynamic hydrogen bonding forms photoresponsive AIE organogels exhibiting reversible structural and luminescent changes.

### Coordination-driven assemblies and nanostructures
**Description:** Metal–ligand interactions furnish therapeutic nanoplatforms and photoswitchable constructs with enhanced functionality.

## Physical strategies
**Description:** Fabrication by doping or encapsulating photoresponsive AIE molecules within preformed polymer matrices.

### Doping and encapsulation into polymer matrices
**Description:** Composite films and particles improve mechanics and stability while retaining robust aggregate-state emission.

### Processing and additive manufacturing methods
**Description:** Drop-casting, spin-coating, electrospinning, and 3D/4D printing produce scalable photoactive AIE materials.

## Charged AIE systems: design and architectures
**Description:** Approaches introducing inherent charges to tune AIE photophysics, assembly, and function.

### Rotor-rich ionic cores and donor–acceptor scaffolds
**Description:** Decorating ionic centers or charging neutral AIE backbones promotes AIE and red-shifted emission.

### Counterion selection and exchange
**Description:** Anion choice modulates solubility, packing, toxicity, and radiative or nonradiative decay pathways.

### Polyelectrolyte architectures and single-chain aggregates
**Description:** Linear and hyperbranched charged polymers improve processability, photosensitization, and film fluorescence.

### Supramolecular ionic frameworks and cages
**Description:** Electrostatic assembly yields porous, stimuli-responsive lattices with tunable luminescence and sorption.

### Characteristic interactions in charged systems
**Description:** Donor–acceptor coupling, anion–π+ contacts, and electrostatics govern emission, ISC, and targeting.

## Photochemical responsive systems
**Description:** Light-triggered chemical reactions induce structural changes or environmental reactivity for macroscopic responses.

### Photoisomerization-driven materials
**Description:** Azobenzene and α-cyanostilbene enable gel–sol transitions and reversible photochromism under irradiation.

### Ring-opening/closing photochromism
**Description:** Spiropyran and diarylethene toggle emissive states for sensing and information encoding.

### Photolysis and degradable systems
**Description:** UV-induced cleavage and photoacid generation afford irreversible patterning and sterilization functionalities.

### Photosensitization for ROS generation
**Description:** Azonia heterocycles and related donors enhance intersystem crossing to efficiently produce reactive oxygen species.

## Photophysical responsive systems
**Description:** Control of radiative and nonradiative relaxation routes to direct light-to-signal or heat conversion.

### Radiative decay pathways for bright emission
**Description:** Restricted motions suppress quenching, enabling strong fluorescence and occasional phosphorescence in aggregates.

### Nonradiative photothermal conversion
**Description:** Rotor-mediated vibrational relaxation yields localized heat for therapy, textiles, and desalination.

### Photoacoustic signal generation
**Description:** Thermoelastic expansion from rapid heating produces deep-tissue acoustic imaging contrast.

## Applications of AIE materials
**Description:** Diverse sensing, patterning, biomedical, energy, and imaging use-cases leveraging aggregate-state emission.

### Chemosensing and analyte detection
**Description:** Light-modulated emission and FRET enable selective, sensitive detection in aqueous environments.

### Photopatterning and optical data storage
**Description:** Rewritable and permanent fluorescent patterns arise from reversible and irreversible photoreactions.

### Photodynamic therapy and antimicrobial treatment
**Description:** ROS-generating systems ablate tumors and eradicate drug-resistant bacteria with spatiotemporal control.

### Photothermal conversion applications
**Description:** Materials support tumor ablation, solar-driven water evaporation, and heat-managing wearable fibers.

### Photoacoustic imaging
**Description:** High-efficiency nanoagents provide enhanced 3D visualization via strong nonradiative energy conversion.

### Photobiosynthesis enhancement
**Description:** Chloroplast-conjugated AIEgens convert unused light to boost electron transfer and ATP production.
